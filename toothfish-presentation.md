# Proposed Research on Toothfish (Dissostichus spp.)

## CCAMLR Conservation Measure 24-01, Paragraph 3

### Chilean Research Plan for Subareas 48.2 and 48.3A

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Overview of Research Plans

-   **Two separate but complementary research proposals:**
    -   Subarea 48.2 (Northern and Southern Research Blocks)
    -   Subarea 48.3A
-   **Duration:** Three fishing seasons (2025/26 - 2027/28)
-   **Research vessel:** FV GlobalPesca I
-   **Primary focus:** Patagonian toothfish (D. eleginoides) and Antarctic toothfish (D. mawsoni)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Key Research Rationale

**Significant data gaps exist in both areas:**

-   No data collected in Subarea 48.2 since 2019
-   No data collected in Subarea 48.3A since 2005

**Current understanding limited by:**

-   Lack of standardized research surveys
-   Lack of updated biological data
-   Need for relative abundance estimates

**Research supports CCAMLR's conservation objectives**

**Strategic importance for Chile:**

-   Establish meaningful presence in CCAMLR fishing research
-   Contribute to sustainable management of Antarctic marine resources
-   Build scientific expertise and capacity in Antarctic marine resources
-   Strengthen Chile's position as a responsible fishing nation in Antarctic waters

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Research Objectives

**For both research areas:**

-   Obtain relative abundance estimates for toothfish by depth strata
-   Characterize size and age structure and mean length of toothfish
-   Characterize reproductive/maturity indices and species ratios
-   Continue the CCAMLR Toothfish Tagging and Recapture Program
-   Characterize bycatch species
-   Document interactions of seabirds and marine mammals with fishing operations

## Survey Design

**Standardized approach for both areas:**

-   **Stratified random sampling (StRS)** with proportional allocation
-   Three depth strata: 600-1000m, 1000-1500m, 1500-2000m
-   Two-stage cluster sampling for biological data
-   Consistent gear configuration (trotline, 2,500 hooks per set)
-   Statistical rigor: Coefficient of Variation (CV) of 12% (or precision target of ±300 kg/2500 hooks at 95% confidence)

## Survey Timing and Duration

Total Survey Duration: 135 days (4.5 months)

**Subarea 48.2:**

-   Fishing season: no restrictions
-   Survey period: March 1 - May 20 (each season)
-   Duration: 81 fishing days (including Weather/maintenance delays)
    -   64 stations in NRB (\~43 days)
    -   34 stations in SRB (\~22 days)
    -   \~1.5 stations per day

**Subarea 48.3A:**

-   Fishing season: from 16 April to 14 September
-   Survey period: from May 26 - July 5
-   Duration: 40 fishing days (including Weather/maintenance delays)
-   48 stations
-   \~1.5 stations per day

## Proposed Catch Limits

**Subarea 48.2:**

-   Total: **188 tonnes**

    -   123 tonnes allocated to NRB (65.3%)
    -   65 tonnes allocated to SRB (34.7%)

-   Based on historical CPUE with 50% recovery estimate since 2019

-   **Note**: 75 tonnes for each research block (150 tonnes total) CCAMLR’s trend analysis (2023)

**Subarea 48.3A:**

-   Total: **76 tonnes**

-   Based on historical CPUE with moderate (50%) recovery assumption

-   Includes 25% precautionary buffer

-   **Note**: No catch limit since 2006 (catch limit was 10 tonnes in 2005)

-   **Note**: Catch limits for adjacent areas were 698 tonnes (48.3B) and 1,629 tonnes (48.3C)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Biological Data Collection

**For both research areas:**

-   Minimum 13 fish measured per longline (Type II samples; e.g., length measurements)
-   Minimum 5 fish measured per longline (Type III samples; eg., otoliths)
-   Sampling intensity of 7 fish per 1,000 hooks
-   Sampling intensity based on protocols from CCAMLR
-   Full bycatch documentation and VME monitoring
-   Seabird and marine mammal observations

**Comprehensive biological sampling program:**

**Subarea 48.2:**

-   Expected Type II samples: 1,274 toothfish
-   Expected Type III samples: 490 otoliths
-   Expected total samples: 1,764
-   Expected Type II samples: 980 Macrourus spp
-   Tagging program: 5 fish per tonne (48.2)

**Subarea 48.3A:**

-   Expected Type II samples: 624 toothfish
-   Expected Type III samples: 240 otoliths
-   Expected total samples: 864
-   Expected Type II samples: 480 Macrourus spp
-   Tagging program: 1.3 fish per tonne (48.3A)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Research Timeline

**For both research areas:**

-   **2025/26 season:** Initial surveys (pilot phase)
-   **2026/27 season:** Continued surveys with methodology refinements
-   **2027/28 season:** Final surveys and comprehensive data collection
-   **2029:** Final synthesis report and management recommendations

**Regular reporting:**

-   Preliminary results to WG-SAM (June each year)
-   Comprehensive reports to WG-FSA (September each year)
-   Research reviews to SC-CCAMLR (October each year)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Vessel and crew:**

-   FV GlobalPesca I
-   Total crew: 49 members
-   **5** scientific observers (including 1 international)

**Supporting institutions:**

-   Instituto de Fomento Pesquero (IFOP)
-   Instituto Antartico Chileno

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Expected Research Outcomes

**Scientific contributions:**

-   First standardized abundance estimates for both research areas
-   Updated biological parameters for toothfish populations
-   Documentation of sex ratios across size classes and depths
-   Improved understanding of population structure and dynamics
-   Enhanced knowledge of VMEs and ecosystem interactions
-   Data for future stock assessments and management decisions

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Alignment with CCAMLR Objectives

**Research directly supports CCAMLR's mission by:**

-   Addressing critical data gaps in data-limited fisheries
-   Following established CCAMLR protocols for research activities
-   Continuing the CCAMLR Tagging and Recapture Program
-   Supporting precautionary management approaches
-   Facilitating international scientific collaboration
-   Minimizing ecosystem impacts through proven mitigation measures

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Conclusion

-   Two well-designed research plans addressing significant data gaps
-   Standardized methodology for robust, comparable results
-   Conservative catch limits to minimize ecosystem impacts
-   Complete alignment with CCAMLR conservation objectives
-   Results will substantially improve toothfish management knowledge
-   Request approval for implementation in 2025/26 fishing season
-   Strategic importance for establishing Chile's presence in CCAMLR research and fisheries
